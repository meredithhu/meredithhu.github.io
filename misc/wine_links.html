The most productive preparations for me were:

  1. Recording my own wine study podcasts: one episode for every region, reading off my own summaries (ended up ~300 pages) of all the study materials I consolidated including WSET diploma text books, GuildSomm study guide, Wine Atlas, and Sotheby's. NYC wine bars and stores in NYC --- my reviews:

  1. Wine bars:
    1. Aldo Sohm: excellent wine list, 9pm pours, and the somms there love to geek out with you!
    2. Terrior: by the one and only Paul Grieco, the menu/wine list is fun, informative, and excellent!
    3. La Compagnie des Vins Surnaturels: great wine list, fun blind tasting gimmick!
    4. Racines NY: for the sake of the lengendary Pascaline. All natural biodynamic. Tuesday wine flights are awesome!
    5. Casellula: features wine and cheese - solid wine list, they appear to have quite some favorite producers, which show up in the list again and again and again --- they are fantastic producers for sure, but I would prefer more variety.
  2. When ordering large quantities, I usually search on wine-searcher and do my homework in terms of where to order from. Here are a few places around NYC that I usually end up ordering from, based on variety of selection and pricing:
    1. Saratoga Wine Exchange
    2. Wine Library
    3. Cellaraiders
    4. 67 Wines
    5. Bedford Wine Merchants
    6. PJ Wine
    7. Gary's Wine and Marketplace
    8. Empire Wine
    9. Tribeca Wine Merchants
  3. Picking up bespoke bottles within NYC, here are a few places:
    1. Chamber Street Wine: David Lillie's natural wine store, best of its kind!
    2. Discovery Wine: natural biodynamic wines, best of its kind!
    3. Astor: the largest and the most comprehensive, region/type discounts every Tuesday/Wednesday/..., some mega tastings are awesome.
    4. Union Square Wine: smaller but a bit more boutique than Astors. I personally frequent their tasting events. Their Sake, Georgian, German, and California selections stand out to me.
    5. Flatiron Wine: smaller but even more boutique than Union Square. Their selections are awesome, old-world focused.
    6. Sotheby's Wine: vast selection of blue chip producers from all over the world, and they are the only distributor for books like Inside Burgundy, Inside Bordeaux in US.
    7. Sherry-Lehmann® Wines: solid selection overall, somewhere in-between UNS and Flatiron.
  4. But the best finds are all from auctions! I love Winebid among several other online auction websites.
